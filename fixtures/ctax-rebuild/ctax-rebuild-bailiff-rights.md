# Council tax arrears: your rights when an enforcement agent (bailiff) attends

When an enforcement agent ("bailiff") visits about council tax arrears, you keep specific
statutory protections. This page is curated guidance, not legal advice or a payment
instruction.

## Right to request Breathing Space
You can ask about the Debt Respite Scheme ("Breathing Space"), which can pause enforcement
and contact while a debt adviser works with you [source: ctax-breathing-space-2024].

## Entry rights summary
An enforcement agent generally cannot force entry into a home for council tax debt on a
first visit, and cannot enter through anything other than a normal means of entry (a door)
without a prior controlled-goods agreement [source: taking-control-of-goods-regs-2013].

## What to do now
Stay calm, do not feel pressured into an on-the-spot payment, and contact the council's
recovery team and a free debt adviser. Emergency support and the council's hardship route
may apply [source: ctax-hardship-policy-2025].
